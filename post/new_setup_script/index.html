Script your enviroment

We all have some sort of bespoke customizations in our various configurations files on our local machines. However big or trivial they might be, couple of functions defined in your shell configuration, or your personal scripts that automate some of the work you do often, or your sweet, sweet configs for Vim or Emacs or whatever other editor you favour.

Well I’ve grown quite used to that stuff on my computer, so everytime a connect to a new server and attempt do something, very quickly I let out a quite audible uuugh when I realise any of those configurations are missing. At that point even the simplest of tasks become a lot less fun, and it’s a pain to perform those tasks localy and then rsync them up, and it’s even more of a pain to install all the missing packages and copy the configurations over.

So I keep quite a bit of my home directory in a Git repository. Vast majority of the stuff I do is in some Git repo anyway, so why not all of my configs as well.
